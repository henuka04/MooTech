Project Scope

Literature Survey

Lumpy skin disease (LSD) is a viral disease that affects cattle, characterized by the appearance of nodules on the skin and causing severe economic losses.

The sharp decline in local milk production, especially in regions where dairy farming is vital, threatens both community livelihoods and agricultural sustainability. This decrease stems from outdated monitoring systems, limited access to modern technologies, and the reliance on traditional methods that no longer meet modern farming challenges. Without real-time data for herd management, farmers struggle to optimize operations and address emerging issues. Outdated infrastructure and limited extension services further hinder progress.

To combat these challenges, this research proposes a customized monitoring system for local dairy farmers. It will integrate advanced technologies like precision agriculture and sensor-based monitoring. Precision agriculture uses advanced analytics to optimize farming practices, while sensor-based monitoring provides real-time data on the health of individual cows.

The main goal is to bridge traditional and modern methods, improving the sustainability and resilience of local dairy farming. This involves creating a comprehensive IoT-based system with three key components. First, IoT devices like wearable belts will collect data such as temperature, heart rate, and movement, alerting farmers to potential health issues in real time. Second, machine learning (ML) will analyze health, environmental, and feeding data to predict milk production and optimize feeding regimes. Third, image processing and deep learning will help identify common diseases, providing early detection and expert consultation.

This system will empower local farmers by enhancing herd health, improving productivity, and providing expert advice, ultimately ensuring the future sustainability of the dairy industry.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

The current use of IoT and health monitoring systems in agriculture is on the rise globally, but the adoption in Sri Lankan dairy farming remains in its early stages. Research conducted in developed countries has shown that IoT can significantly enhance livestock tracking and health monitoring. However, these systems often overlook the unique challenges faced by smallholder farmers in Sri Lanka, such as limited access to reliable internet and a lack of expertise in managing complex IoT networks. This study aims to address these issues by designing a simple, region-specific system that enables local farmers to identify and classify health conditions in dairy cows without requiring advanced technological knowledge.

Severity assessment

Another gap in existing IoT systems for livestock management is the limited integration of historical health data. While many systems provide real-time health updates, they often lack the capability to link to an animal's historical health status. This absence hinders the ability to assess the severity of ongoing health issues and make long-term decisions based on health trends. This research will develop a solution that generates detailed health profiles for each cow, enabling farmers and veterinarians to better assess the severity of health problems by reviewing historical data, thus improving disease management and intervention strategies.

Information sharing

Inadequate remote monitoring and the need for on-site veterinary presence are significant drawbacks of current systems. Many rural farmers in Sri Lanka lack immediate access to veterinary services. This research seeks to close that gap by introducing a mobile application that enables remote monitoring and information sharing between farmers and veterinarians. Real-time data will be accessible remotely, allowing veterinarians to diagnose and provide consultations without being physically present. Additionally, the system will focus on scalability and economic feasibility, ensuring that it can grow with the needs of smallholder farmers while remaining affordable through low-cost hardware and software solutions.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can the integration of IoT, machine learning, and image processing technologies enhance the productivity and sustainability of the dairy farming industry in Sri Lanka?

The dairy farming sector in Sri Lanka is facing a severe decline in milk production, which threatens farmers' livelihoods and the overall sustainability of the agricultural economy. This decline is largely due to outdated herd management practices and a lack of modern agricultural technologies. Current methods fail to provide real-time data and actionable insights, preventing farmers from effectively addressing challenges and improving productivity. Furthermore, the limited access to modern tools, coupled with outdated infrastructure, exacerbates the issue, making it difficult for farmers to adapt to changing economic and environmental conditions.


Proposed Solution

The solution to the decline in local milk production requires the development of a comprehensive system that integrates advanced technologies such as IoT, machine learning, and image processing into dairy farming practices, tailored specifically to the needs of Sri Lankan farmers. IoT devices will be deployed to continuously monitor the health of dairy cows, providing real-time data on vital indicators like temperature, heart rate, and movement patterns, which will enable early detection of health issues and timely interventions. Machine learning will analyze this data to generate predictive insights, optimizing feeding schedules, forecasting milk production, and identifying the onset of common diseases. Image processing will further enhance disease detection by using cameras to monitor for visual signs of illness, enabling non-invasive and automated health assessments. The system will be designed with scalability and affordability in mind, ensuring that even smallholder farmers with limited financial resources and internet access can adopt and benefit from it. It will also include remote monitoring and consultation capabilities, allowing farmers in rural areas to access veterinary expertise and receive timely advice through mobile applications. By improving herd management, boosting productivity, and ensuring long-term sustainability, this solution will empower local farmers to overcome the challenges posed by outdated practices, contributing to the resilience of the dairy farming sector and ensuring its future viability.

Research Objectives

Integration of Advanced Technologies:

Implement iot devices such as wearable belts for cows to collect vital data like temperature heart rate and movement develop a mobile app that provides real time alerts and health status updates for individual cows.

Predictive Analytics for Milk Production:

Utilize machine learning algorithms to process health data environmental factors and feeding patterns forecast milk production and quality allowing farmers to optimize feeding regimes and improve production levels.

Early Disease Detection:

Apply image processing and deep learning techniques to identify common diseases in dairy cows such as Lumpy skin develop software to analyze visual data and detect early signs of diseases facilitating timely intervention.

Bridging Traditional and Modern Approaches:

Provide training and support to farmers on the use of new technologies helping them transition from traditional to modern farming practices create a platform for farmers to access expert advice and information from veterinarians and agricultural specialists.

Sustainability and Resilience:

Enhance the overall sustainability of dairy farming by improving herd health and productivity build the resilience of local agricultural economies by equipping farmers with innovative tools and insights to navigate economic and environmental challenges.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed IoT-based real-time health monitoring system for dairy cattle in Sri Lanka consists of three main components aimed at improving milk production, ensuring early disease detection, and enhancing overall herd management.

  • IoT-Based Health Monitoring

    • IoT devices will be integrated into the dairy farming process through wearable sensors placed on the cows. These sensors will continuously collect data on vital health indicators such as body temperature, heart rate, and movement patterns. This real-time data will be transmitted to a central system where it will be analyzed, providing immediate insights into each cow's health. Farmers will receive alerts in case of any abnormalities, allowing them to take preventive measures before the cow's health deteriorates.

  • Machine Learning for Predictive Analytics

    • The system will utilize machine learning models to analyze the health and environmental data collected from the IoT devices. These models will generate predictive insights into milk production, quality, and potential health risks. By analyzing historical and real-time data, the system can forecast milk yield, identify patterns that suggest illness, and recommend optimal feeding schedules or breeding times. This will allow farmers to optimize their operations based on data-driven decisions, improving productivity and efficiency.

  • Image Processing and Deep Learning for Disease Detection

    • Cameras and image processing software will be employed to monitor the physical condition of cows, focusing on identifying common diseases such as mastitis and foot-and-mouth disease. Through deep learning algorithms, the system will analyze visual data to detect early signs of illness, ensuring that farmers can act swiftly to prevent the spread of disease within their herds. This component will also include an interface that enables farmers to consult with veterinarians remotely, sharing health data and images for expert guidance.
